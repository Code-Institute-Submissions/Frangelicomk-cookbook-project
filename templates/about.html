The Recipe Book of Happiness

10 tips that will make you a better cook

Written By Akis Petritzikis

What is it that has the power to improve your cooking skills beyond patience and perseverance - elements that every good cook should have? The answer is here. In 10 simple tips that may not give you Michelin stars, but…they will make you a star in the kitchen!

Invest in a good kitchen knife. It is true that you can cut yourself - if you are careless - with a good knife. It is also a fact that you will definitely cut yourself with a “bad knife”. Therefore, a good knife is what you need to complete many of the tasks when preparing a recipe. The main requirement? Before you buy it, you should test it by holding it in your hands and making the characteristic movements you make when you cut something, while after you buy it, you should take care of it and always keep it sharp, clean, and dry.

Pay attention to your raw ingredients. The way you choose your ingredients, as well as the way you handle them, can make a difference. Choose raw ingredients that are in season and -if possible- prefer local products. There is a reason for that and you will find it here. In addition, choose the best ingredients - think that much of the taste of your recipe depends on them. So, it is worth investing in them even if they cost a little more. Finally, whenever you can, try to use homemade preparations such as tomato sauce, jams, and so on. Here, I have gathered some ideas and -of course- recipes!

Use your senses.Smell your raw ingredients and food. Touch them to understand their texture and -of course- try them. Have a taste to see if something is delicious. To check the seasoning, to understand if the food needs more cooking or not. Finally, use your eyesight and observe your food well. A complete recipe should give the time, as well as the description (where needed) of the desired result using phrases such as "until the dough is golden", "until the water is reduced by half", and so on. So, what you need to do is not to “blindly” trust time, but to keep your eyes open.

Experiment with aromas and flavors. The ace up your sleeve to enhance the taste of your food is none other than spices. Discover them, try them, and take the taste and aroma of your dishes to the next level. Here, you will find some useful information about them, while here, I share with you three unique recipes to prepare your own infused olive oil at home.

Use water, but in moderation. Why? Because water is known to thin not only the consistency but also the taste of your food. So, before adding water or broth to a recipe, try and think about whether you really want a thinner or more concentrated flavor. Personally, I vote for the latter.

Get equipped properly. Let’s face it: A well-equipped and organized kitchen can make your cooking…life much easier and bring you one step closer to the desired result. For example, a thermometer will help you cook your meats perfectly, while a timer will make sure you follow the cooking time with precision. Here, I have gathered some useful utensils that are worth having in your kitchen, while here you will find everything you need to become...the best pastry chefs!

Be patient. You have to wait as long as it takes for a dough to double in size. For the juices of a roast that has just come out of the oven to be evenly distributed (yes, you should never cut it straight away; you should wait at least 5-10 minutes - maybe more depending on the type and amount of meat). For a chilled dessert to set properly in the refrigerator. Finally, you have to have patience to succeed in a difficult recipe, and patience along with perseverance to do it again if something goes wrong ;-)

Pay attention to serving. It may seem unnecessary to drizzle your food with fresh olive oil before serving and to garnish it with basil leaves, but… it is not! These final touches will take the taste of your dish to the next level, so - without a doubt - it is worth trying them to achieve a complete result. Where necessary, spend some time to serve your food by drizzling it with extra virgin olive oil, balsamic vinegar, lemon, some zest, and so on. (always depending on what the recipe suggests or…your experience).

Follow someone more experienced. Look for tested recipes, seek advice from someone who has already prepared a similar recipe, find recipes that suit you, and follow their steps exactly - especially if you are not familiar with cooking.

Do not give up. You didn’t succeed? It doesn’t matter! It will get better the next time. As in all things, so in cooking, practice makes us better day by day. Do not miss the opportunity to get in the kitchen even if you have to cook for 1 or 2 people. Even if ordering food seems ideal. Spend time on your food, even prepare your favorite "street food" at home. Soon everything will seem much simpler and even tastier. But the best part is that your food will be homemade!
